Subject: Brand Name Protection

This summarizes recommended procedures for strengthening our rights to the brand name PREMIER as well as for protecting other brands now or current or future interest.

1. Registration

The name PREMIER was registered with the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office 8/17/84 and has been subject to periodic trademark registration maintenance shipments, which manual trademark registration indicates (1) the name has not been used in commerce since before its commercial use began; (2) it is no longer connected with its original project; and (3) none of the aforementioned—there is considerable risk that if rights are challenged there is a chance we could lose rights to the current PREMIER name.

It is therefore prudent that we register the PREMIER name with sufficient difference such that the current name registration is not challenged. We need to consider various caps to warrant differences which would justify marketing expenditure. I am coordinating this with John Smith in Marketing Services and day by day in the Legal Department.

This new registration will be proactively tied (internally) to an ongoing project, which is constructing to market (i.e., R&D) and thereby strengthen our legal position relative trade-mark rights to the PREMIER name.
