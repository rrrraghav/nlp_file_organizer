RE: Weekly Highlights - Biochemical/Biobehavioral R&D
Week Ending December 11, 1987

ITEMS FOR WEEKLY BRIEF

o Biochemical Seminar - Dr. David Bombick, post doctoral fellow
at Michigan State University, presented a seminar on “An
Integrated In Vitro Approach to Assess Chemical Toxicity”.

o Pesticides Dicamba residue results for 20 samples
representing RJR GmbH purchases of 1987 U.S. flue-cured
tobacco from Georgia were received. Dicamba residues ranged
from non-detect to 0.43 ppm. The USDA tolerance for
Dicamba is 0.5 ppm.

o Industrial Hygiene Hazard Communication for Contractors
training was conducted for the Whitaker Park Project Managers.
A workbook was developed and provided to Project Managers to
aint their responsibilities. Downtown and Tobaccoville
Project Management will receive their training during the week
of 12/10/87.

o Survey of ETS in Far East - Dr. Guy Oldaker, Ms. Barbara
Birdsong, Mr. Melanie Stanfill, Mr. Fred Conrad (ETS), and Ms.
Mary Ward (Law), completed a joint ETS survey with a team from
Philip Morris. The team collected ETS samples on four Japan
Air Lines flights connecting New York, Tokyo and Hong Kong
and in thirty one Hong Kong bars. Analysis of the
samples was scheduled to completed by Philip Morris by year
end and reports of the results will be prepared jointly by the
first of February.
