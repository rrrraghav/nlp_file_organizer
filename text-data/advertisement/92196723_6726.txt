WRITERS OF FICTION: Tomorrow
is the deadline for the seventh
annual Providence Journal Short Story
Contest. Stories may be left at the journal
office, the entrance to the downtown
library, or mailed. No entries will
be accepted after that.
One summary and one or two
copies of the winning story is re-
quired for publication. The prize
is $500. This stories will be pub-
lished in the Providence Journal
Magazine.
The stories must be original
copies of fiction, unpublished in
any form. They must
the authors live in New
England.
Employees of the Providence
Journal Co. and members of their
immediate families are not eligible
to compete.
Stories must be typed and
double-spaced. Stories longer than
3000 words if typed on 12 space
are disqualified. They may be ac-
companied by a telephone number
and daytime telephone number
and address.
The story writer may affix
his signature to the front page of the
story. The person who
the story will not be notified.
