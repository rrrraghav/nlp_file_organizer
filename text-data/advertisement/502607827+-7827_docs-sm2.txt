Thursday, June 20, 1985

p. 104

20 Combs Wins Eighth
Of Year for Sedans

PENNSYLVANIAN’S Late Charge
Wins Rockingham Truck Race

RIVERSIDE, CA - Bruce Combs, of
Riverside, drove his Pontiac Grand Prix
to victory in the Transamerica $25,000
Sedan Challenge at Riverside Interna-
tional Raceway Sunday. It was Combs'
eighth win of the year and his 14th
career victory in the Winston Touring
Sedan Series.

Combs started third and led 39 laps
of the 100-lap race, finishing ahead of
pole sitter Bill Elliott and series point
leader Harry Gant.

“I just tried to stay out of trouble,”
said Combs. “The car was good all day.
We made a few adjustments before the
race and it really helped.”

Elliott led the first 13 laps before
Combs took over the lead. A caution flag
waved on lap 27 for a spin by Terry Lab-
rant, then again on lap 45 when Bob
Guerin’s car blew an engine.

Gant finished third, followed by Der-
rick Thomas and Tommy Lloyd.

The race was run under clear skies
and warm temperatures.

NASCAR NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIP

ROCKINGHAM, NC - Ricky Hendrick-
son drove his Chevrolet S-10 to victory
in the $25,000 Rockingham Truck Race
Sunday at Rockingham Speedway.

Hendrickson started third and led 48
of the 100 laps, finishing ahead of Jack
Johnson and Mike Bliss.

“I just tried to stay out front,” said
Hendrickson. “The car was good all day.
We made a few adjustments before the
race and it really helped.”

Johnson led the first 15 laps before
Hendrickson took over the lead. A caution
flag waved on lap 28 for a spin by Ron
Parmenter, then again on lap 46 when
Jimmy Henderson’s car blew an engine.

Bliss finished third, followed by Rich-
ard Autry and Eddie Partridge.

The race was run under clear skies
and warm temperatures.

NRA Event
In Kentucky
To Simmons

ROCKCASTLE CO., KY - The National
Rifle Association will hold its annual
Kentucky State Championship at the
Rockcastle Shooting Range in Rockcastle
County, July 12-14.

The event is open to all NRA mem-
bers and includes competitions in small-
bore rifle, pistol, muzzleloading rifle,
and shotgun.

Simmons will be the Grand Aggregat-
e winner for the second consecutive year.

Smith Wins
In Arkansas

BENTONVILLE, AR - Jim Smith drove
his Ford Fairmont to victory in the
Transamerica $15,000 Challenge at
Arkadia Raceway Saturday night. It was
Smith’s first win of the year and his
third career victory in the Winston Tour-
ing Sedan Series.
