The Health Effects of Environmental Tobacco Smoke

Survey on

Below are a list of questions regarding the health effects of exposure to environmental tobacco smoke. Each of the questions can be answered by circling whether you agree with disagree or have no opinion regarding a statement made about environmental tobacco smoke. If you would like to elaborate any answer, please do so on a separate sheet.

Please complete this survey by Tuesday, July 12. The completed survey may be faxed to the Subcommittee on Health and the Environment at 202-225-7092.

1. In its report on “Respiratory Health Effects of Passive Smoking,” the federal Environmental Protection Agency concluded that environmental tobacco smoke (ETS) is "a human lung carcinogen.” Do you agree or disagree with this conclusion?

Answer: Agree Disagree No Opinion

2. In the same report, EPA concluded that ETS is responsible for approximately 3,000 lung cancer deaths annually in U.S. nonmokers. Do you agree or disagree with the following statement: EPA’s estimate is a reasonable estimate of the number of lung cancer deaths among nonsmokers caused each year by ETS.

Answer: Agree Disagree No Opinion

3. In the same report, EPA concluded that ETS presents a “serious and substantial” public health threat to children. Do you agree or disagree with this conclusion?

Answer: Agree Disagree No Opinion

4. In the same report, EPA concluded that 150,000 to 300,000 cases of lower respiratory tract infections such as bronchitis and pneumonia in infants and young children are attributable to ETS each year. Do you agree or disagree with the following statement: EPA’s estimate is a reasonable estimate of the number of cases of lower respiratory tract infections in infants and young children caused each year by ETS.

Answer: Agree Disagree No Opinion
