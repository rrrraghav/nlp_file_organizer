VETERANS ADMINISTRATION
WASHINGTON 25, D. C.

This is an appeal to you to cooperate in a scientific
study which will almont certainly yield results of great im-
portance to medicine and public health.

The rapid increase in the use of tobacco in recent
years has raised questions about its effects on health. We are
studying the possible effects of tobacco on health, the evidence
suggests that it may be harmful. This is a large and com-
plex study requiring the cooperation of many. It involves
asking you certain questions to obtain a dependable answer.

Consequently the Veterans Administration is cooperat-
ing with the Public Health Service in a study
of tobacco use among veterans.

Only a few minutes of your time will be required to
complete this questionnaire. Your answers will be con-
fided for your convenience and will not be used for any
purpose other than for the completion of this medical study.

With many thanks for your cooperation,
Sincerely yours,

W. Alig Bay
