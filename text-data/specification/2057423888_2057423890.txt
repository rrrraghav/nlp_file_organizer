HEALTH HAZARD DATA

ACUTE EFFECTS
IF SWALLOWED, INHALED, OR ABSORBED THROUGH SKIN,
CAUSES BURNS, EXTREMELY DESTRUCTIVE TO TISSUE OF THE MUCOUS MEMBRANES
AND UPPER RESPIRATORY TRACT. EYES AND SKIN: INFLAMMATION AND EDEMA
MAY DEVELOP.  EXPOSURE MAY RESULT IN PNEUMONITIS, PULMONARY EDEMA.
SYMPTOMS OF EXPOSURE SHORLD INCLUDE BURNING, HEADACHE, COUGHING, AND
 WHEEZING. LABORATORY ANIMALS SHOW BREATHING DIFFICULTY, NASAL
 BLEEDING, AND BRONCHITIS.  EYES: PAIN, PHLYCATHE, NEUROLOGICAL
 EFFECTS IN MAN MAY NOT HAVE BEEN ADEQUATELY INVESTIGATED.

FIRST AID
EYE CONTACT: IMMEDIATELY FLUSH EYES WITH LARGE AMOUNTS OF WATER FOR
AT LEAST 15 MINUTES WHILE HOLDING EYELIDS OPEN. GET IMMEDIATE MEDICAL
ATTENTION.
SKIN CONTACT:  IMMEDIATELY WASH THE AFFECTED AREA WITH SOAP AND PLENTY
OF WATER. REMOVE CONTAMINATED CLOTHING AND SHOES. GET MEDICAL ATTENTION.
INGESTION: DO NOT INDUCE VOMITING UNLESS DIRECTED BY PHYSICIAN. GIVE
LARGE AMOUNTS OF WATER OR MILK.  GET IMMEDIATE MEDICAL ATTENTION.
INHALATION: REMOVE TO FRESH AIR. IF BREATHING IS DIFFICULT, GIVE OXYGEN.
IF NOT BREATHING, START ARTIFICIAL RESPIRATION. GET IMMEDIATE MEDICAL
ATTENTION.

PHYSICAL AND CHEMICAL HAZARDS

THIS MATERIAL REACTS VIOLENTLY WITH WATER.  IT MAY ALSO REACT
VIOLENTLY WITH ALCOHOLS, AMINES, AND STRONG OXIDIZING AGENTS.

PREVENTIVE MEASURES

WEAR APPROPRIATE PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT: SAFETY GLASSES OR GOGGLES,
RUBBER GLOVES, APRON.  USE IN A WELL VENTILATED AREA.  AVOID CONTACT WITH
WATER AND OTHER INCOMPATIBLE MATERIALS.
