SECTION III -- HAZARDOUS IDENTIFICATION DATA *
MEDICAL CONDITION(S) AGGRAVATED BY EXPOSURE: ASTHMA, BRONCHITIS, ASPIRATION
AND OTHER RESPIRATORY PROBLEMS.
SYMPTOMS OF EXPOSURE:
EYES: SEVERE IRRITATION OR BURNS
SKIN: BURNING SENSATION, PAIN, REDNESS
INHALATION: COUGHING, THE RESPIRATORY SYSTEM FROM KIDNEY INJURIES
INGESTION: BURNS TO MOUTH AND STOMACH

SECTION IV -- FIRST AID MEASURES *
EYES: FLUSH WITH WATER FOR AT LEAST 15 MINUTES. GET MEDICAL ATTENTION.
SKIN: WASH WITH SOAP AND WATER. REMOVE CONTAMINATED CLOTHING.
INHALATION: MOVE TO FRESH AIR. ADMINISTER OXYGEN IF NECESSARY.
INGESTION: DO NOT INDUCE VOMITING. DRINK LARGE QUANTITIES OF WATER.
GET MEDICAL ATTENTION IMMEDIATELY.

SECTION V -- FIRE FIGHTING MEASURES *
FLAMMABILITY LIMITS: N/A EXTINGUISHING MEDIA: WATER SPRAY, DRY CHEMICAL,
CARBON DIOXIDE, APPROPRIATE FOAM. SPECIAL FIREFIGHTING PROCEDURES: WEAR
SELF-CONTAINED BREATHING APPARATUS AND PROTECTIVE CLOTHING.

SECTION VI -- ACCIDENTAL RELEASE MEASURES *
EVACUATE AREA. CONTAIN SPILL. VENTILATE AREA. ABSORB WITH DRY MATERIAL
AND DISPOSE OF PROPERLY.

SECTION VII -- HANDLING AND STORAGE *
STORE IN COOL, WELL-VENTILATED AREA. KEEP CONTAINER TIGHTLY CLOSED.
AVOID CONTACT WITH METALS, ORGANIC MATTER, AND OTHER INCOMPATIBLE MATERIALS.

SECTION VIII -- ENGINEERING CONTROLS *
LOCAL EXHAUST VENTILATION.

SECTION IX -- PERSONAL PROTECTION *
RESPIRATORY: USE NIOSH/MSHA APPROVED RESPIRATOR.
VENTILATION: GOOD GENERAL ROOM VENTILATION.
EYES: CHEMICAL GOGGLES OR FACE SHIELD.
SKIN: PROTECTIVE CLOTHING AND GLOVES.

SECTION X -- STABILITY AND REACTIVITY DATA *
STABLE UNDER NORMAL CONDITIONS. AVOID CONTACT WITH INCOMPATIBLE MATERIALS.
HAZARDOUS POLYMERIZATION: WILL NOT OCCUR.
