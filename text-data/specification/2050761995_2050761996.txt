SECTION VI – Health Hazard Data
Route(s) of Entry: Inhalation, Skin Contact, Eye Contact, Ingestion
Eye Contact: Causes severe irritation.
Skin Contact: May cause mild irritation. Prolonged or repeated contact may cause dermatitis.
Inhalation: Irritating to respiratory tract.
Ingestion: May be harmful if swallowed.

SECTION VII – Precautions for Safe Handling and Use
Ventilation: Adequate ventilation should be maintained.
Eye Protection: Safety glasses with side shields are recommended.
Skin Protection: Wear appropriate gloves.
Respiratory Protection: Not normally required.
Other Protective Equipment: Eye wash stations and safety showers should be available in the work area.

SECTION VIII – First Aid Measures
Eye Contact: Flush immediately with large amounts of water for at least 15 minutes, and seek medical attention.
Skin Contact: Wash affected area with soap and water.
Inhalation: Remove to fresh air. If breathing is difficult, administer oxygen and seek medical attention.
Ingestion: Do not induce vomiting. Seek medical attention immediately.

SECTION IX – Storage and Handling
Store in tightly closed containers in a cool, dry, well-ventilated place away from incompatible materials. Keep away from heat, sparks, open flame.
