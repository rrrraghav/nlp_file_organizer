IN REVIEW…IN REVIEW…

Today, the U.S. HOUSE Agriculture/FDA Subcmte reported a $13.6 billion FY98 spending bill for the FDA. The subcmte appropriated $15.2 million for FDA enforcement of the first phase of the regulations...FDA had requested $34 million.

The U.S. Senate Judiciary Cmte will begin a series of hearings tomorrow to review the terms of the agreement between the State Attorneys General and the tobacco industry. A hearing on the agreement’s legal implications is set for 7/16 and another on public health issues. Fort Mills, Mississippi Attorney General Moore and National Center for Tobacco-Free Kids Must Ayres are scheduled to testify tomorrow.

On 6/24, U.S. REP. BISHOP (D-GA) introduced HR2017, the “Tobacco Use By Minors Reduction Act of 1997.” The bill seeks to prohibit tobacco sales to persons under the age of 18. It was referred to the Energy and Commerce Cmte.
