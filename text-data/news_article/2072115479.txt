The cigarette excise tax hike
was proposed by Gov. Anthony Earl
to help balance the budget. The
increase would bring in $40 million
more for the state next year.

The governor said he wanted the
tax increase to offset cuts in other
programs, but lawmakers have
said they want to use the money for
property tax relief.

“We’re not going to take out of
the general fund and put into prop-
erty tax relief,” said State Rep.
Robert L. Brown, D-Milwaukee, chair-
man of the Joint Committee on Fin-
ance. “That’s just not going to hap-
pen.”

The governor also proposed a 15-
cent per pack increase in the cig-
arette excise tax.

Wisconsin Manufacturers & Com-
merce is lobbying for a reduction in
the corporate income tax rate, which
is currently 7.8 percent. The com-
pany would like to see the rate cut
to 5 percent.

“We believe that reducing the cor-
porate income tax rate will stimu-
late economic growth and create
jobs,” said company lobbyist John
W. Immel.

Wisconsin Bell Inc. Co., is lob-
bying for legislation to allow the
company to offer a new service,
which would involve transmitting data
over telephone lines.

“We believe that this service will
benefit consumers by providing them
with more choices,” said company
lobbyist James A. Stuber.

Northwestern Mutual Life Co., is
also lobbying for legislation to allow
the company to offer a new type of
insurance policy.

“We believe that this policy will
benefit consumers by providing them
with more protection,” said com-
pany lobbyist John R. Stuber.

Wisconsin Power & Light Co., is
lobbying for legislation to allow the
company to build a nuclear power
plant.

“We believe that this plant will
provide Wisconsin with a clean and
reliable source of energy,” said
company lobbyist William J.
Wegner.
