failed to comply with an order

marketing activities. The case
has been adjourned until July 21.

June 17, 1957

TOBACCO FIRM’S
WHITE CLASH
LUNG CANCER CASE

Lake Placid, June 16 -
The American Tobacco Co.
won a $20,000 judgment to-
day in Federal Court when a
jury decided that the death of
Arthur S. White was not caused
by smoking cigarettes.

Mrs. White had alleged
that her husband, who smoked
two to three packs of cigaretts
a day for 15 years, died as a
result of lung cancer. She
sued the American Tobacco Co.
and its advertising agency,
N. W. Ayer & Son, Inc., of New
York.

The jury found that there
was no evidence to support Mrs.
White’s claim.

TAGES-ANZEIGER
Zurich, Switzerland
June 20, 1957

U.S. Lung Cancer Suit Verdict:
Judge Dismisses Complaint Against Tobacco Firm

Zurich, June 19 - A Federal
Court in Hartford, Connecticut,
has dismissed a suit brought by
Mrs. Arthur S. White against the
American Tobacco Company. Mrs.
White claimed that her husband’s
death from lung cancer was caused
by smoking cigarettes produced
by the defendant firm.

The jury found that there was
no evidence to support this claim.
The American Tobacco Company
is one of the largest tobacco firms
in the United States. It is a sub-
sidiary of the British-American
Tobacco Company, Ltd., which has
its headquarters in London.
