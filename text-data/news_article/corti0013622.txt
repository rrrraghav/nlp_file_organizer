MAY 3 1955

Research Grants
Made up of Smoking

Twentyone new research
grants totaling nearly $118,000
were approved yesterday by the
Motley V. Haguestt, chairman of
the Tobacco Industry Research
Committee.
The grants, he said, bring
to over $690,000 the amount
now available for support of
independent research projects
relating to tobacco and health
and for medical student fellowships.
The committee has a $1,000,000 fund from which
these grants are made.
“The need for expanded re-

more evident every day,” he
said. “The cause of lung can-

ever increasing research into
this ailment. Scientific proof is
lacking that smoking or any
other single factor is the cause.
