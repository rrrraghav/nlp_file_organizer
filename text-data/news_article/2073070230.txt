Utahns offered shot at country dancing stardom

Marlboro Country Nights Dance Showdown, the nation’s largest and richest country dance contest, is staging preliminary rounds throughout Utah and Arizona in the United States. The amateur grass-roots country dance competition will be held in 13 cities, from to be held Nov. 7 in Tucson, Ariz., from there, winners will advance to the national finals scheduled for Nov. 22 in Dallas.

Winners at the club championship level advance in the regional champi-
onship, to be held Nov. 7 in Tucson, Ariz., from there, winners will advance to the national finals scheduled for Nov. 22 in Dallas.

The competition is open to couples, age 21 years of age, or older, who are currently active as dance instructors in a studio or studio.

Entry forms are available at The New Sandy’s Station. Each competition will include three contestants, each to be judged on their ability to dance the required two-step, waltz and swing.
