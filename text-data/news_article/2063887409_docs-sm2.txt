Yorkshire Evening Post, 20th December 1994, Page 4

‘PASSIVE-SMOKING RISK SMALL’

THE amount of tobacco smoke inhaled from passive smoking is very small, according to a study carried out by a Yorkshire research team.

Scientists at Sheffield University in Europe in Harrogate suggest that the average person inhales less than one cigarette a week as much as possible.

The survey was sponsored by the Centre for Health Organisation funded principally by the tobacco industry but receiving some money from tobacco interests.

It claims that more than 100 other sources of pollution from other sources such as car exhaust fumes containing benzene.
