UPI-83 (SMOKING) CIRC
WASHINGTON - U.S. SURGEON GENERAL SAYS CIGARETTE SMOKING
SHOULD BE BANNED IN NEARLY ALL PUBLIC PLACES.
“EVIDENCE IS ACCUMULATING THAT THE NONSMOKER HAS UPON
HIS RIGHT TO THE POLLUTION HIS SMOKER NEGLECTS,” SAID TODAY.
THE SURGEON GENERAL, SPEAKING FROM THE CONFUSED WHITE HOUSE
PLACE, SAID IT IS TIME TO BAN SMOKING AT ALL CONFINED PUBLIC
PLACES SUCH AS RESTAURANTS, THEATERS, AIR LINES, TRAINS AND BUSES.”
HE SAID THE NONSMOKER HAS “AS MUCH A RIGHT” TO CLEAN AIR.
“NONSMOKERS HAVE AS MUCH A RIGHT TO CLEAN AIR AS A PREGNANT WOMAN
HAS TO A HEALTHY CHILD,” HE ADDED. “THE UNBORN CHILD IS HARMED BY
SMOKING.”
THE SURGEON GENERAL MADE THE STATEMENT IN ADVANCE OF A REPORT
DUE OUT NEXT WEEK FROM THE INTERAGENCY COUNCIL ON SMOKING AND HEALTH.
THE REPORT WILL SAY THAT CIGARETTE SMOKING IS ASSOCIATED WITH
A VARIETY OF ILLNESSES, INCLUDING FETAL MALFORMATIONS.
