ST. LOUIS PROFESSOR GETS
TOBACCO INDUSTRY GRANT

Dr. John P. Wyatt, professor
of pathology at St. Louis Uni-
versity’s School of Medicine, has
been awarded a research grant
from the Tobacco Industry Re-
search Committee, it was an-
nounced yesterday.

The grant is for continued in-
vestigation into pregnancy, le-
sion development and effects
on & disease of the lungs.
Wyatt’s group has previously
had to investigate 15 years on re-
search into the causes of cancer.



ANDERSON INDEPENDENT
Anderson, South Carolina
December 15, 1960

Grant Awarded
To Dr. Hester

MC CORMICK - Dr. Lawrence
L. Hester, son of Mr. & Mrs.
L. L. Hester, has been award-
ed a research grant by the To-
bacco Industry Research Com-
mittee.

The grant will permit him to
continue his investigation into
the relationship between the use
of tobacco and pregnancy. Dr.
Hester is professor of medicine
and director of the Department
of Obstetrics and Gynecology at
South Carolina Medical Col-
lege.

Dr. Hester is one of 11 scien-
tists receiving such grants, which
total nearly $300,000.



CHEMICAL & ENGINEERING NEWS
Easton, Pennsylvania
December 19, 1960

Nearly $300,000 has just been
awarded in Tobacco Industry Re-
search Committee grants to sci-
entists at 11 institutions. The re-
cipients and their projects are:
Dr. John P. Wyatt, St. Louis Uni-
versity, Mo., $35,000 for studies of
the effects of cigarette smoking on
pregnancy; Dr. Lawrence L. Hester,
South Carolina Medical College,
$35,000 for continued investigation
of the relationship between cig-
arette smoking and pregnancy;
Dr. Alvin J. Strouse, University of
California, San Francisco, $40,000
for studies on the effects of cig-
arette smoke on lung tissue; Dr.
Robert F. Swope, University of
Rochester, N. Y., $35,000 for in-
vestigation of the effect of cig-
arette smoking on cardiovascular
function; Dr. William C. Cooper,
University of Colorado Medical
Center, Denver, $40,000 for stud-
ies of the effects of cigarette smok-
ing on pulmonary function; Dr.
James F. Follows, University of
Utah, Salt Lake City, $35,000 for
studies of the effect of cig-
arette smoking on respiratory
disease; Dr. Charles C. Lingrel,
University of Louisville, Ky.,
$40,000 for studies of the effects
of cigarette smoking on carbo-
hydrate metabolism; Dr. John
W. Hollingsworth Jr., Medical Col-
lege of Virginia, Richmond, $35,-
000 for studies of the effect of
cigarette smoking on blood clot-
ting; Dr. Robert A. Kyle, Univer-
sity of Kansas City, Mo., $40,000
for studies of the effects of cig-
arette smoking on serum proteins;
Dr. Edward F. Ennis, University of
Buffalo, N. Y., $35,000 for stud-
ies of the effect of cigarette smok-
ing on the central nervous sys-
tem; Dr. William E. Sothor-
ton, Baylor University, Dallas,
$40,000 for studies of the effects
of cigarette smoking on the heart.

The Tobacco Industry Research
Committee is composed of repre-
sentatives of 15 tobacco companies.
