JERSEY JOURNAL
Jersey City, New Jersey
October 26, 1961

Doctor Wins
Study Grant

Dr. Christopher M. Martin
of Seton Hall College of Medicine,
has been awarded a $5,000 grant
for research on the effects of
tobacco smoke on the lungs.

The grant was made by the
Tobacco Industry Research Com-
mittee. Dr. Martin will study
the effect of tobacco smoke on
the respiratory tract and its
effect on lung tissue.

Lansing, Michigan
October 24, 1961

Scientist
Gets Grant

Tobacco Institute O.K.’s
Study of Nicotine

Michigan State University
scientist will study how
tobacco plants produce nicotine
with a $10,000 grant.

The tobacco in-
dustry research committee.

The research
is the fourth in as many years
for Richard H. Byrum,
professor of chemistry, widely re-
garded as an authority on nic-
otine. He will study the process
by continuing a study technically
called “Biosynthesis of Nicotine.”

Byrum is a member of the faculty
of the Institute which helps firms de-
velop new tobacco products. He is
the author of more than 100 re-
search papers and has been listed
in Who’s Who in Science.

He will study how plants con-
vert amino acids into nicotine, a
process that occurs only in to-
bacco and related plants.

The grant was awarded by the
Tobacco Institute, which is fi-
nanced by major tobacco firms.
