Tobacco Man Rolls His Own



100357592




TIMES
Hartford, Connecticut
March 6, 1962





Getting in shape for a 350th anniversary celebration to be held
in Jamestown, Va., May 16-19, is John Rolfe, above. He’s a descendant
of the man who first cultivated tobacco as a cash crop.  A permanent
human exhibit will show how he grew it and introduced it to Europe.
The event will mark the 350th anniversary of the successful crop
of tobacco in America.



JAMES TOWN, Va., will celebrate 350th anniversary of tobacco in the U. S. May 16-19
with a special pageant. Among the characters recreated for the event will be John Rolfe
and Pocahontas, both of whom played important roles in the early history of Virginia.
