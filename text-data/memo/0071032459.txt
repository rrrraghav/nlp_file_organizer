SUBJECT Nicotine Production: Suggestions for an Exploratory Program.

1. Introduction:

In my memorandum of 21 May, 1963, "Possible sources of nicotine", I compared the merits of obtaining nicotine from a specialty crop of N. rustica as against producing it from stalks left in the field. The conclusion was that if nicotine were to be produced it should be produced from a crop grown for that purpose. Recently, one more current publication which explores this very question in some detail and confirms our preliminary conclusions.

On the basis of the results presented it becomes possible to formulate a pilot program which is presented below for your consideration.

2. Summary of Prior Field Trials:

a. Experiences of the U.S.D.A., covering the period 1929-1938, in growing tobacco as a source of nicotine are collected in U.S.D.A. Technical Bulletin No. 820, June 1942. This publication is available in the A.T.C. library under catalog No. T-7445 hence only the pertinent highlights will be reiterated here.

The comparative tests were conducted in field plots widely scattered around the country, including plots in Virginia (Bowling Green and Arlington), Pennsylvania, Wisconsin, Missouri, Oregon, California, North Carolina, Tennessee and a few others. As a result a wide variety of soil and climatic conditions were compared.
