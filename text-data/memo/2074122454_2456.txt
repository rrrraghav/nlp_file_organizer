Subject: July 17 Hearing Held Before Senate Committee on Labor and Human Resources on Preventive Health and Health Promotion.

Sen. Hatch - simply introduced Gillespie.

John Gillespie, Pres., Georgetown Consultants.

Discussed deficiencies in health cure system and need
for change in lifestyle. Addressed important role of voluntary
groups and personal initiative. No discussion of smoking.
Hatch asked him if there should be more federal funding
or development of private funding. Gillespie responded
that we have depended too long on Federal Government and
must increase private money.

Panel 1 – helping the individual.

• William Fooge, Center for Disease Control, HHS.

Discussed health risk appraisal - what people can
do to change behavior toward a more healthier life style.
Says cigarettes cause over 320,000 premature deaths each year.
This caught Hatch’s attention. (“Is this true?”)
But, says Fooge, smoking is decreasing.

• John Naughton, School of Medicine, State University
of New York.

Discussed exercise and health. Says regular physical
activity successful in reducing incidence of clinically
manifest coronary heart disease. Concurrent with the
introduction of exercise is anti-smoking campaign. Says
inactivity in itself is a risk factor but that smoking
is a stronger risk factor.

• Walter Menninger, Menninger Clinic.

Discussed what can be done to motivate self care. Says
pleasure principle is basic motivating force - to make
life easier. Also the wish to be in control of one’s
life and a reality impulse when one's efforts for pleasure
or autonomy are frustrated. Not practiced in discipline care
