AMERICAN MEDICAL ASSOCIATION HOUSE OF DELEGATES

Resolution: 22
(A-67)

Introduced by: Ohio Delegation
Subject: Smoking and Health
Referred to: Reference Committee E
(H. B. Anderson, M. D., Chairman)

Whereas, Cigarette smoking has been established as having a causal relationship with death and disability from lung cancer, coronary heart disease, emphysema and chronic bronchitis; and

Whereas, Death and disability from these diseases cause needless pain and anguish and millions of dollars in lost wages, medical expenses and hospitalization; end

Whereas, The conclusion of the Smoking and Health Advisory Committee to the Surgeon General of the United States Public Health Service is that:

"Cigarette smoking is a health hazard of sufficient importance in the United States to warrant appropriate remedial action.";

therefore be it

Resolved, That members of the American Medical Association, officially recognize their opportunities and responsibilities in this important field by setting an example as regards cigarette smoking, and by participating actively in their practices and in community programs to influence and persuade people not to smoke.
