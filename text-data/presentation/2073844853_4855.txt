Coalition of 75 Organizations Launches National Petition Drive
to Have Tobacco Regulated by the Food and Drug Administration

Washington, Oct. 4 – A coalition of more than 75 national health, medical, consumer and
religious organizations announced a national petition drive and public awareness campaign today to
bring tobacco products under the jurisdiction of the federal Food and Drug Administration.

"We’re taking this campaign to the nation's front door," said Scott D. Ballin, chairman of the
steering committee for the Coalition on Smoking OR Health, comprising the American Heart
Association, the American Lung Association and the American Cancer Society.

“As a result of this petition drive, members of Congress and other national policymakers are
going to hear directly from the people they serve. The tobacco companies should be held
accountable for how they make their products, how they advertise and promote them to children and
what they hide from the public about the addiction and the diseases caused by smoking,” said Ballin.
The diverse coalition of organizations, ranging from the American Medical Association to the
Interreligious Coalition on Smoking or Health, and representing more than 10 million Americans, has
joined together under the name, “Citizens to Protect Our Children From Tobacco.” Volunteers and
staff from the organizations will circulate petitions calling on Congress, President Clinton and the
Food and Drug Administration, "to ensure that tobacco products are regulated without banning
them, including the way they are manufactured, sold, labeled, advertised and promoted." The
petition also urges those named to ensure that “the public’s health is protected from the dangers of
tobacco use to the greatest extent possible," and to ensure that "children are not encouraged to
