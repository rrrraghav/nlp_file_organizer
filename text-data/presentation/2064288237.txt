PA EPA TO EXPLAIN PROPOSED OZONE, PARTICULATE MATTER STANDARD

FOR RELEASE: FRIDAY, DECEMBER 6, 1996

EPA TO EXPLAIN PROPOSED OZONE, PARTICULATE MATTER STANDARDS IN NATIONAL SATELLITE BROADCAST

EPA officials will hold a detailed question and answer session on its proposal for health standards for ground-level ozone(smog) and particulates in a national satellite broadcast Monday, Dec. 9, from 1-3 p.m. EST. The broadcast will include: overview of the standards; implementation issues update; explanation of the scientific review process; and welfare effects; presentations by EPA experts; EPA’s rationale for the proposal; and instructions for further information. The broadcast is presented as a public service by EPA's Air Pollution Distance Learning Network. For location and space reservations at the nearest viewing site: 1) call Jean Taylor at 919-541-4946 or see Internet site http://134.67.104.12/ntt/api/APTC.htm and choose either document JUNE-96_WPS or JUN-96_WPS TXT; 2) to view the broadcast through the National Technological University, call Anne Riker at 703-956-8429 or e-mail at anne@mail.NTL.edu; and 3) for Air National Guard sites call Janeya Riggs of EPA’s Office of Air Quality Planning and Standards at 919-541-2353. There are no phone calls or video feeds outside the viewing sites—participants must go to viewing sites. Videotapes of the broadcast will be available afterwards for $25.00 through North Carolina State University at 919-515-4268. For Washington, D.C. area pass, there are no local viewing sites (be sure to call or fax before hand to reserve space). US EPA Headquarters, contact Gene Padgett at 202-260-2071(phone) or 202-260-7037(fax); and DC Resources Management Division, contact Barbara Willens at 202-643-5180(phone) & 202-643-5102(fax).
