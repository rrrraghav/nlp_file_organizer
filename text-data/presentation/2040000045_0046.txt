NEW YORK, NY, June 20, 1995 -- Jack A. Hammond, a Senior Vice President of
Westvaco Corporation and Manager of the company’s Bleached Board Division, today
issued the statement noted below. Westvaco is a major manufacturer of paper,
packaging and chemicals.

Philip Morris U.S.A. should be commended for decisive action to resolve taste
and odor problems which prompted its May 26 recall of cigarettes. Today, the
company noted that it had erred when it suggested that the presence of trace
amounts of MTC in its products might cause temporary discomfort.

Westvaco is Philip Morris U.S.A.’s principal supplier of bleached board
packaging for cigarettes. As one component of its bleached board manufacturing
processes, Westvaco formerly used an FDA-approved preservative called chlora.
Minute traces of methyl isothiocyanate (MTC) may derive from thions. There are no
health risks associated with these tiny trace amounts.

Because Westvaco wishes to eliminate any possible lingering concern as a
result of Philip Morris’ initial comments made on May 26, we have discontinued the
use of chlora, which has been widely used in paper and other industries for over 20
years. We have substituted isothiazolin, another widely used FDA-approved
