SMOKE-FREE CLASS OF 2000
GRADUATES FROM SECOND GRADE

In ten years a special class of students, the Smoke-Free Class of 2000, will
graduate from high school and become the first smoke-free generation in America.

On May 2 & 3 these future ambassadors will graduate from their second year
in the Smoke-Free Class of 2000 project, a 12-year initiative of the American
Heart Association, American Lung Association and American Cancer Society. One
hundred second graders from the Chicago area and another 100 from central
Illinois will take part in a special graduation being held at 10:00 a.m. on May
2 at the State of Illinois Center and on May 3 at the steps of the Illinois
State Capitol Building in Springfield. These children, symbolic of the SFC 2000
project in Illinois, will pledge their commitment to remaining smoke-free and
encouraging others to do the same.
