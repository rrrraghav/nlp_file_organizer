ATLANTA CONVENTION AND VISITORS BUREAU’S

“ACCOMMODATING OUR INTERNATIONAL VISITORS” SEMINAR

PREPS MEMBERS ON PROTOCOL

Nineteen Atlanta-based international government representatives equip Atlanta's hospitality industry with protocol tips to accommodate influx of international guests

Atlanta, March 7, 1996 -- With the Olympic Games 134 days away, The Atlanta Convention and Visitors Bureau (ACVB) is preparing Atlanta’s hospitality industry for the influx of international visitors by co-sponsoring the “Accommodating Our International Visitors” Seminar. The seminar's prestigious panel is comprised of 10 representatives from Atlanta-based consuls general’s offices who will educate the attendees on the cultural mores and customs of their respective countries. Nine additional international government representatives will round out the distinguished list of participants available to field any protocol inquiry.

Featured panelists include consuls generals from Argentina, Belgium, France, Germany and Mexico; consuls from Britain, Greece and Switzerland; the consul senior from Japan and the deputy president from the Nigerian-American Chamber of Commerce.

The nine additional international government representatives attending the seminar include the consul general of Costa Rica; the consuls from Colombia and The Netherlands; the trade commissioners from Australia, Austria and Panama; the director of public relations from Canada; the public affairs officer for Quebec and the assistant to the director general for Taiwan.
