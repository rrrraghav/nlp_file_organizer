the Tobacco Institute. With me are Dr. George E. Schafer, a con-
sultant to the Institute, and Richard F. Kingham, of Covington &
Burling, counsel to the Institute. We appreciate this opportunity

The Board is considering an array of proposals ranging
from a total ban on smoking aboard airplanes to the complete
elimination of CAB regulation of in-flight smoking. Based on the
Board's experience with the existing regulations, as well as the
unequivocal congressional mandate for deregulation of the airlines,
the choice is clear: revocation of regulations governing smoking
aboard airplanes and leaving smoking, like other matters of pas-
senger preference, to the managerial discretion of the airlines,
acting in response to competitive market forces.
