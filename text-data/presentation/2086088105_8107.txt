INTRODUCTION

The Belleville News-Democrat commissioned Regional Research and Development Services (RRDS), a public service and applied research unit of Southern Illinois University at Edwardsville, to conduct a market research study. The purpose of the study was to accurately and objectively measure the perceptions of people in the News-Democrat area regarding the News-Democrat and other publications, their readership and buying habits, and to identify meaningful advertising sources. The following describes the methods used to conduct the study and to systematically evaluate the responses.

METHODOLOGY

The study was based on a telephone survey of the Belleville News-Democrat Retail Trade Zone consisting of zip code areas detailed in Table 2 (Sample Configuration Demographics). (The information in this report is based on a smaller cross section of the Retail Trade Zone.)

The survey area designated as the Zone with zip codes of newspaper readership. Yesterday, last week, last Sunday, last four Sundays, last Wednesday, and other standard readership questions; buying trends, interests, and shopping habits; advertising and newspaper sources, geographic conditions and demographic information. The questionnaire was exhaustively pretested and revised to eliminate potentially biased, misleading and difficult to understand questions. Regional Research Development Services (RRDS) adheres to standard and objective research methods, to maintain the integrity and accuracy of research products when conducting applied and survey research.
