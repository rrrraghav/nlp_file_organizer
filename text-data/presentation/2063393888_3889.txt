PHILIP MORRIS FILES MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT
AGAINST THE FOOD AND DRUG ADMINISTRATION

NEW YORK, October 15, 1996 -- Philip Morris Inc., Coyne Beahm, Inc.,
Brown & Williamson Tobacco Corporation, Lorillard Tobacco Company and R.J.
Reynolds Tobacco Corporation today filed a Motion for Summary Judgment
against the Food and Drug Administration to prevent the agency from pursuing
its illegal assertion of jurisdiction over tobacco products. The motion was filed
in the U.S. District Court for the Middle District of North Carolina.

“Our opposition to the FDA’s rule rests not with its stated goal of reducing
 underage tobacco use, a goal we share,” said Steven Parrish, Senior Vice
President, Corporate Affairs, Philip Morris Companies Inc. “We vigorously
object to the FDA's specious and arbitrary interpretation of federal law, which
FDA claims gives it broad powers, including the power to ban cigarettes
altogether. This is contrary to the clear intent of Congress, which has never
granted the FDA authority to regulate the tobacco industry.”

“Our Motion for Summary Judgment demonstrates why the FDA’s attempt
to regulate tobacco products is a contrived mix of absurdity and contorted logic,”
said Mr. Parrish.
