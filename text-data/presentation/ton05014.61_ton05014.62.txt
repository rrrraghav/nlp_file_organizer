TOBACCO COLLEGE

ACCIDENTAL FIRE ISSUE

Primary Responsibility: Lisa Osborne

Objective: To provide students with an overview of the
accidental fire issue -- the U.S. fire
problem, the feasibility of a "self-
extinguishing" cigarette, and TI's fire
prevention program.

Timing: Forty-five minutes.

Materials: FireCare, Fire Safety Education Evaluation
Survey,"New Tools" kit, FireSense, “Smoke
Detectors Save Lives” bus poster, Inter-
national and Barriers (if available) reports,
and Pluggie, the fire safety hydrant.

Overview: 1. The first 10 minutes will be devoted to
an explanation of the "self-extinguishing"
cigarette issue and tobacco industry response
to the U.S. fire problem.

2. A 20-minute description of TI's fire
prevention program will include selected
program products and examples of materials
from recent grants.

Q&A

Outline: 1. Brief overview of the issue and intro-
duction of speakers.

2. Background on "self-extinguishing" issue
and preliminary findings of the Technical
Study Group.

3. Update on international studies and the
“Barriers” report.

4. Description of TI fire prevention
program -- selected program/materials and most
recent grants including FireCare, FireSense,
FSSE, “New Tools” kit, Juvenile Firesetter
training program, and smoke detector campaigns
in D.C. and San Francisco.
