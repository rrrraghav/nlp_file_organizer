Philip Morris Fills

Two Latin Posts

Dr. Juan Antonio Cordido-Freytes,
Jr., has been elected president of C.
A. Tabacaleras Nacionales, Philip Mor-
ris International’s affiliate in Ven-
ezuela.

Dr. Cordido, a well-known figure
in Venezuelan economic and legal
circles, began his association with
Tabacaleras Nacionales more than 10

years ago as legal counsel. He was
named to the board of directors
in 1960 and became a vice president
in 1961.

Allen A. Schumer has been ap-
pointed general manager of Tabaca-
leras Centroamericanas, S.A., Philip
Morris International’s Guatemalan
affiliate. Prior to this promotion, he
had been assigned to other operations
of Philip Morris International in
Latin America.

Before joining Philip Morris in
1961, he was associated in a sales
capacity with the Revlon Overseas
Corp.

Lorillard Names New
Tomp Division Manager

S. P. Weaver has been appointed
division manager in Tampa, Fla., by
P. Lorillard Co. He was formerly
assistant division manager in Miami.

Taking Dr. Weaver’s place in
Miami is C. L. Goodwin, formerly
assistant division manager in Cam-
den, N. J.

The appointments were among the
simultaneous promotion of 21 mem-
bers of P. Lorillard Co.’s tobacco
sales organization in February.

Lorillard Posts Record Earnings

P. Lorillard Co. has announced
record net income of $35,819,000 or
$2.64 a share for the year ended
Dec. 31, 1961, compared with $27,-
885,000 or $2.03 a share in 1960.

Net sales were $452,423,000 com-
pared with $385,675,000 in the pre-
vious year.

Edens Promotes
Two in Louisville

Brown & Williamson Tobacco Corp.
has promoted two manufacturing ex-
ecutives in its Louisville, Ky., branch.
Named to the newly created posi-
tion of manager-manufacturer operations
is Joseph R. Edens, formerly
manager-Louisville branch. It will be
his responsibility to assist the vice
president in planning and providing
for the efficient operation of B&W’s
factories.

Robert M. Hayward, who has
served as factory manager-Louisville
operations, succeeds Mr. Edens as
manager-Louisville branch.

Horillard Has
Record Earnings

P. Lorillard Co. has announced
record sales and record earnings in
1961. Net sales were $452,423,000
up to $385,675,000 for 1960.  Net
income totaled $35,819,000 or $2.64
a share, compared with $27,885,-
000 or $2.03 a share in the pre-
vious year.

E.R. Woodford Jr., president of
P. Lorillard Co., said that the com-
pany’s record performance was due to
increased volume and favorable price
adjustments, as well as improved
operating efficiency.

“Our cigarette brands continued to
gain in popularity throughout 1961,”
Woodford added. “We are confident
that this trend will continue.”

The company’s board of directors
has declared a quarterly dividend of
$1.25 a share on the preferred stock
and 60 cents a share on the common
stock, payable March 15 to holders
of record January 31.

B&W Unveils New
Wings with Filter

Brown & Williamson Tobacco Cor-
poration has changed its economy-
priced Wings brand from a king-
sized plain to a king-sized filter
type cigarette.

There will be no change in the
price of this brand, the company
said for the moment that Wings will be
available for about 30 cents a car-
ton.

The new Wings with Filter is de-
signed to appeal to smokers who
desire a mild, economical smoke.

B&W Names
New Regional Sales Mgr.

Brown & Williamson Tobacco Corp.
has appointed James H. Miller re-
gional sales manager for the South-
west region. He will be responsible
for sales in Texas, Oklahoma, Ar-
kansas, Louisiana and New Mexico.

Miller joined Brown & Williamson
in 1957 as a traveling salesman and
was promoted to district sales man-
ager in 1960.  Prior to joining the
company, he was with the Liggett
& Myers Tobacco Co. for eight years.
