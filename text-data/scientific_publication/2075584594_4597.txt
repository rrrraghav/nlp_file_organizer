Plusieurs rapports nationaux et internationaux
ont été publiés depuis 1986 qui
accordent pour conclure que l'exposition à la fumée
ambiante de tabac est un facteur de risque de

cancer du poumon.

ABSTRACT

Exposure to environmental tobacco smoke (ETS)
is widespread in European countries, the
populations of homes, workplaces and public
places. A number of epidemiological studies
have addressed the association between ETS expo-
sure and increased risk of lung cancer among
passive smokers, as well as the increase of respira-
tory illnesses in children; active and passive
smokers. Studies available up to 1985 have been
reviewed by several national and international
authorities, which agreed in including that ETS
exposure is causally linked to lung cancer. At least
12 epidemiological studies have been published
since then, and the association has been fur-
ther strengthened. The possibility of a bias resulting
from a biased definition of ETS exposure
has been considered, but it seems unlikely that
such a model can explain the whole excess risk of
lung cancer associated with ETS exposure.
Such an effect, that may be responsible for
the increased risk of respiratory illnesses in
children is considered supported by the limits
of a causal association is there and active smoke.

The need exists for more precise epidemiological
studies concerning the health effects on popula-
tions exposed to ETS. Estimates are available from
several sources, including the European Economic
Community, which suggest that ETS exposure is
responsible for at least 2% of all lung cancer
deaths in Europe.
