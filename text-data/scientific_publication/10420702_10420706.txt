THE adverse effects of cigarette smoking on health are well established. Much effort has gone into counseling cigarette smokers that they should stop smoking, since the cessation of smoking has been shown to reverse some of the adverse effects of smoking. The person who stops smoking frequently gains weight.
The amount of weight gain is often attributed to increased food intake due to period of tobacco deprivation. Our study in this report aims to determine the metabolic changes associated with the cessation of smoking.

If the cessation of smoking was associated with a significant increase in body weight, it could be that the person who stops smoking has an altered metabolism.

The subjects reported to the laboratory for 12 hours prior to the start of the study. They came to the laboratory and assumed a supine posi-

After this, they breathed through a fresh Douglas bag into a spirometer. The volume of quiet breathing of room air through the apparatus was measured during the first 10 minutes. One hour
