Vol. XLVII AUGUST, 1954 No. 8

THE TWENTIETH POSTGRADUATE
ASSEMBLY

The Twentieth Annual Postgraduate
Medical Assembly of South Texas, held in
Houston July 19-21, will go down as
one of the largest and most pleasing meet-
ings ever held under the leadership of
Dr. Herman H. Dulin, as president, the
officers, the executive committee and the
ladies who so graciously volunteered their com-
mittes, are to be congratulated on this,
the culmination of a year’s work in
effecting all plans and to faithful
carrying out of the program put forth at
that time. The attendance was well up to
expectations, with over 1200 registrants.

The Banquet was held at the Rice Hotel
on Saturday evening, July 21st, with over
900 present. Dr. J. Kenneth Shivers,
President of the Texas Medical Associa-
tion, gave the address.

Several members of the Harris County
Medical Society have been in the forefront
in developing this project to its present
respected state.

The program for this year’s meeting at
the Shamrock Hotel September 23-25 is
outstanding. A long and distinguished
guest speakers are included Dr. Elmer
Hoe, President-Elect of the American
Medical Association; Dr. Kieffer Davis,
President-Elect of the Industrial Medical
Association; Dr. William C. Menninger,
Menninger Foundation; Mr. Gordon Gray,
Chairman of Personnel Security Board
of the Atomic Energy Commission and Former
Secretary of the Army; Dr. John C. Bugner,
Director of the Division of Biology and
Medicine of the Atomic Energy Commis-
sion; Former Governor Val Peterson
Federal Administrator of Civil Defense,
and the Mutual Directors of DuPont, East-
man Kodak, General Electric and New
York Telephone Companies.

The Chamber of Commerce has again
extended an invitation to all members
to attend this conference which reflects
great credit upon Houston’s industry and
medicine. Those desiring more information
concerning the program or registration
may call Mrs. Nancy Palm at LI-3171.
