CURRICULUM VITAE

George A. Santisteban, Ph. D.

A. Personal

George Anthony Santisteban, born April 12, 1918, Canaoas, Sonora, Mexico.
Citizenship, U.S.A.

B. Education

1. Graduate:

University of Utah; 1947-1951.
Ph. D., 1951, School of Medicine.
Major: Human Anatomy, Minor: Vertebrate Zoology. Training in gross
anatomy, neuroanatomy, medical histology, human physiology,
pathology, bacteriology, biochemistry, medical statistics,
cancer biology, hemopoietic tissues, radiology, biology of
connective tissues. Thesis: "The Growth and Involution of
Lymphatic Tissue and Their Relation to Secretions of the
Adrenal Cortex." Major Professor: Thomas F. Dougherty, Ph. D.

Postdoctoral training

1950-1953, research under Dr. Thomas F. Dougherty and John Z. Bowers,
M.D., in cancer biology and radiology at the University of Utah.

1963-1964, special training in microanalytical methods for analysis of
intracellular RNA at the Institute of Neurobiology, Goteborg, Sweden.
Sponsor: Professor Holger Hyden.

1966-1967, Special fellowship, U.S.P.H.S., to continue training and
do research in Goteborg, Sweden. Sponsor: Professor Holger Hyden.

M.A. 1949

Major: Zoology, minor: human anatomy. Thesis: "Histology of the
Hair Follicle in the Long-Tailed Weasel". Major Professor:
W.K. Neuby, Ph. D.

2. Undergraduate:

Pasadena Jr. College; 1938-1940. Pasadena, California.
Major: Prefrofreshy. Training in general forestry, general botany,
geology, surveying, astronomy, economics, history, English,
mathematics, chemistry.

Montana State University; 1940-1942; 1944-1946.
Majors: forestry and zoology. Training in soil technology, range
management, higher mathematics, surveying, dendrology,
silviculture, plant physiology, ornithology, mammalogy,
zoology, embryology, mammalian physiology, animal ecology,
literature physiology, histology, evolution, biological
reproductive physics.
