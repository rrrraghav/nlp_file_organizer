1948-1950: Ph.D. research in indium-115, University of Chicago. Discovery of the
natural β radioactivity of indium-115, its β decay energy and radioactive half-life.

1950-1954: Technical Operations Officer, Armed Forces Special Weapons Project, Department
of Defense. Planning and direction of laboratory and field experiments on nuclear
radiation effects and radioactive fallout at the Nevada Test Site and at Eniwetok and Bikini.

1954-1956: Research Associate, Enrico Fermi Institute of Nuclear Studies, University of
Chicago, heading a research group in studies of radioactive strontium-90 in air and
precipitation; in plants, dairy products and other foods; and in animal and human bone specimens.

1956-1962: Branch Chief and Research Group Head, GS-15, Air Force Cambridge Research
Center, Bedford, Mass. Experimental studies of the atmospheric aspects of global radioactive fallout from nuclear tests.

1962 to present: Research group leader, National Center for Atmospheric Research,
Boulder, Colorado. Experimental studies in atmospheric radioactivity and geochemistry.
Ion-level alpha spectroscopy and absolute β counting. The properties of radioactive
lows and aerosols in the atmosphere and biosphere. The interactions of dynarnics of
ionor radiation progeny aerosols.

1967: Elected to National Academy of Distinguished Scientists of the
U.S. Department of Atomic Radiation, 1967, United Nations Commission on Atmospheric
Investigations and Radioactivity (US-UNHRAM), 1971.

Membership: Health Physics Society; American International Union of Nuclear Studies;
American Geophysical Union; Sigma Xi; Tau Beta Pi; Sigma Zeta; Phi Beta Kappa;
Honorary member, Association of Former Students, U.S. Military Academy.

1963-1964: Consultant, National Academy of Sciences.

1965-1967: Consultant, Atomic Energy Commission.

1967-1971: Consultant, United Nations Environment Programme.

1968-1971: Consultant, Environmental Protection Agency.
