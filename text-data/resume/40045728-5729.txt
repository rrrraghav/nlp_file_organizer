James R. Walker, Professor

Education

1960 B.S., Northwestern State College, Natchitoches, LA (Chemistry/Bacteriology)
1964 Ph.D., University of Texas at Austin (Microbiology)
1965-67 Postdoctoral Fellow, Princeton University, Princeton, NJ (Biochemical Science)

Professional Experience:

1963-65 Bacteriologist, U. S. Army Medical Lab, Europe, Landsstuhl, West Germany
1967-71 Assistant Professor, Microbiology Dept., Univ. of Texas, Austin
1971-72 Associate Professor (without salary), Microbiology Dept., Univ. of Texas
1971-72 Medical School, San Antonionios
1971-78 Associate Professor, Microbiology Dept., Univ. of Texas, Austin
1972-73 Research Associate of Konrad Bloch, Chemistry Dept., Harvard Univ.,
Cambridge, MA
1977 ASM Visiting Professor, Federal Univ. of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
1978-81 Professor, Microbiology Dept., Univ. of Texas, Austin
1981-84 Professor and Chairman, Microbiology Dept., Univ. of Texas, Austin
1993-present Professor, Microbiology Dept., Univ. of Texas, Austin
