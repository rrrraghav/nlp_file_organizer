WILLIAM A. KLOPMAN
SECOND VICE PRESIDENT AT&T

William Klopman is chairman and chief executive officer of Burlington Industries. He was elected second vice president of the American Textile Manufacturers Institute at the Institute’s 1980 annual meeting.

Klopman joined Burlington in 1946 as a sales trainee and has served in various sales and executive capacities. He was president of the Klopman division from 1963 until 1971.

Klopman was appointed group vice president of Burlington Industries in 1971 and executive vice president in 1972. He was elected to the Board of Directors in 1973, and was named president of Burlington April 23, 1974. He was elected chairman, president and chief executive officer September 15, 1976 and held those three titles until February 2, 1978, when a president was elected by the company’s Board of Directors.

He is a member of the Board of Directors of Western Electric Corp., the North Carolina National Bank Corp., the North Carolina National Bank, and the Chamber of Commerce of the United States. He has been chairman of the ATMI International Trade Committee and serves on the Institute's Executive Committee. He is on the Policy Committee of the Business Round Table, and is a member of the Business Council. He is a trustee of the Wesley Long Hospital in Greensboro, N.C.

Klopman is a native of New Jersey, and is a graduate of Williams College.
