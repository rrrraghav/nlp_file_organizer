WEISS, DR. PAUL ALFRED, 45 E. 42nd St., New York 21, N.Y. Biologist.
Vienna, Austria, March 31, 1898; parent. Vienn. Tech. Inst., Vienna, 1916; Ph.D.
Univ. Berlin, 1921. Instructor in Zool. Univ. Wisconsin, 1921-25; assoc. prof. 1925-42; prof. 1942—. Mem. Acad. Sci. Vienna; Austria.
Dir. biol. res. inst. Assoc. Austrian Universities. Sigma Xi. Fellow Wilson Inst., Chester, Pa.
Univ. Berlin, Dr.rer.nat., 1921; Univ. Wisconsin, Sc.D. 1938; Univ. Zurich, LL.D. 1940.
Chm. of committee on biol. standardization, U.S. Natn. Res. Council.
Fellow Scripps Foundation for Biol. Research.
Auth. “The Structure and Development of the Phylum Cnidaria,” “Annelida”, “Invertebrate Zoology.”
Mem. Zool. Verein Wien; Soc. German Natural Scientists and Physicians.
Soc. Austrian Biol.
Biol. Soc. Univ. Wisconsin.
Secr. Wisconsin Acad. Sci., 1930-42.
Consult. U.S. Dept. Agr.
Fellow Guggenheim Foundation.
Lieut. Col. (ret.) U.S. Army.
Married, 1926, Hildegard von Staehlin; two children.
Education: Univ. Vienna, 1914-16; Univ. Berlin, 1916-18; Univ. Wisconsin, 1921-25.
Activities: Research on invertebrate zoology, particularly cnidaria and annelida.
Awards: Univ. Wisconsin Vilas Professorship, 1942.
Addresses: 45 E. 42nd St., New York 21, N.Y.; Dept. of Zoology, Univ. Wisconsin, Madison, Wis.
Interests: Biol. standardization, invertebrate zoology, cnidaria, annelida, marine biol.,
taxonomic research, animal development, comparative anatomy, physiology, ecology,
biogeography, evolution.
Member: Zool. Verein Wien; Soc. German Natural Scientists and Physicians; Soc. Austrian Biol.; Sigma Xi; Wisconsin Acad. Sci.; Univ. Berlin; Univ. Zurich.
Auth.: “The Structure and Development of the Phylum Cnidaria,” “Annelida”, “Invertebrate Zoology.”
Associate: Assoc. Austrian Universities, Vienna, Austria.
Fellow: Scripps Foundation for Biol. Research, La Jolla, Calif.
Lieut. Col., U.S. Army (ret.).
Consultant: U.S. Dept. Agr.
Guggenheim Fellow.
