CURRICULUM VITAE OF DR. EMIN BIDWELL WILSON

Dr. Edwin Bidwell Wilson, Professor of Vital Statistics Emeritus
at Harvard University, and one of the worldâ€™s outstanding scientists
and experts in the field of vital statistics, was born in Hartford,
Connecticut, on April 25, 1879.

A graduate of Harvard University, Dr. Wilson received his Ph.D. at
Yale University and also attended L'Ecole Normale Superieur, The
Sorbonne and the College de France. In 1955, Wesleyan University
(Middletown, Connecticut) conferred upon him the degree of LL.D.

Dr. Wilson is a Former President (1927-31) of the American Academy
of Arts and Sciences and also (1929) of the American Statistical
Association. After receiving his degrees, Dr. Wilson taught
successively at Yale University, Massachusetts Institute of
Technology and Harvard. He was Professor of Vital Statistics at
Harvard from 1922 until 1945, when he became Professor Emeritus.
He was Vice President of the National Academy of Sciences for the
term 1945-53 and has been the Managing Editor of its proceedings
since 1945.

Dr. Wilson was President of the Social Science Research Council
from 1929-31, and from 1937 to 1942 he was Chairman of the American
Society for the Control of Cancer. He has been a long-time member
of the Advisory Board and Committee of Selection for the
United States and Canada of the John Simon Guggenheim Memorial
Foundation, and he was a member of the Science Committee of the
National Resources Planning Board, 1935-42. He was Stevenson
Lecturer on Citizenship at Glasgow, Scotland, in 1946. Since
1940 he has been a Consultant at the Office of Naval Research in
Boston. Dr. Wilson is an honorary life member of the Board of
Directors of the American Cancer Society.
