A BRIEF BIOGRAPHY OF THOMAS S. OSDENE

Thomas S. Osdene was born in Prague, Czechoslovakia in 1927.

Educated in London, England, Dr. Osdene received his Bachelor of Science degree in Special Chemistry with first-class honors from Birkbeck College, the University London in 1951 and also received the Collegiate Prize in Chemistry. He became a research chemist at the Chester Beatty Institute of Cancer Research, University of London, where he worked while pursuing his doctorate. He earned his Ph.D. in Organic Chemistry from the University of London in 1955. He came to the United States in 1955 for postdoctoral studies at Princeton, followed by a staff appointment to the University of Texas H. D. Anderson Hospital and Tumor Institute. He taught at Baylor College of Medicine before joining the Wyeth Laboratories Division of American Home Products Corporation for six years as a senior research scientist and group leader.

Dr. Osdene has served on the editorial boards of the Year Book of Cancer (1956-1976) and Index Chemicus (1968-69). He is a member of the Chemical Society (London), the American Chemical Society, and the Society of Sigma Xi. In 1973, he presented the John W. Watson Lecture at the Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University. He has been a member of the Research Advisory Council of Virginia Commonwealth University since 1983.

Dr. Osdene joined Philip Morris in 1955. He was appointed Manager of the Chemical Research Division of the Philip Morris Research Center in June, 1965. In July, 1969, he was appointed Director of Research. In 1980, he was appointed Director of Research and Extramural Studies. In 1984, he was appointed Director of Science and Technology, reporting to M. Serrano in New York.

Since 1970, he has served in the Smoke Study Group of CORESTA, the Cooperation Center for Scientific Research Relative to Tobacco. From 1976 to 1980, he was a member of the Scientific Commission of CORESTA. He was re-elected to the Scientific Commission in 1984, and named Vice President. In 1985, he was elected as President of the Scientific Commission. The First American to be so elected.

He has been a member of the Board of Trustees of Battelle since 1986. He also served as President of Smoke Study from 1987 to 1990. He resigned from The Bakursforsching International since 1990.
