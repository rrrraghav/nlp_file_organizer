On the Petition of Thomas & Samuel Harris Comittee
to his Majestys Councill

The said Petioners humbly represent that they are
poore Inhabitantâ€™s and have been soe for many yeares
and have always paid all due Taxes & Rates imposed upon
them as such.

That their sd Land is situated in the County of Albemarle
& is bounded by the Lands of John Early, William
Goodwin & others.

That they are possessed of said Land under a Lease
from Coll: Ludwell Lee Esqr: which Lease hath been
in force for these many yeares past & that their sd
Lease doth containe a Rent reserved of one Bushell
of Wheat per acre yearly.

That the said Coll: Ludwell Lee Esqr: is now become
a Councillour in his Majestys Councill & hath
therefore noe time to attend to the Collection of
the Rents due from them, which doth occasion them
great Inconvenience.

That they are willing to pay all such Rents as are
due from them according to the tenor of their sd Lease
if they were duly demanded by Coll: Ludwell Lee Esqr:
or his Agent.

Wherefore they humbly pray that their Lordship will be
pleased to grant them a Warrante for one thousand Acres
of Land within this Colony, as an encouragement to
them to settle & improve the same.

And as in duty bound they subscribe themselves
The Petioners 

Thomas Harris
Samuel Harris

By Mr: John Ambrister their Agent

Albemarle County Virginia
the 29th of June 1763

To his Majestys Councill

Please to consider the case of your
Petitioners & order as you see cause.

John Ambrister at request of the Petioners
hath subscribed this for them.

All future Rent due granted.
