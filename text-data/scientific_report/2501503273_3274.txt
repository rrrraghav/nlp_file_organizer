CYTOKERATIN (CK) EXPRESSION
IN RAT LUNG AND LUNG TUMORS

Satoage, W.K., Bains, H., Piresstini, D., Kuen, M., and Terceira, A., Institut für Strahlenschutzforschung, Freisinger Str. 3, 85319 Kißlegg (F.R.G.)

A. Objectives
1. To establish a method for analysis of CK in rat lung paraffin sections
2. Compare CK expression in different types of spontaneous rat tumors

B. Background information available:
CK expression patterns in cultured-cells and rat lung tissue (Terceira et al., 1993)

C. Materials and Methods
1. Tissues
a) Normal Lung:
8 adult rats (Wistar), euthanized by CO2 inhalation
b) Spontaneous Lung Tumors:
10 adult rats (Wistar), euthanized by CO2 inhalation
11 tumors:
1 adenomat alveolar carcinoma
2 fibrolamellar carcinoma
3 squamous cell carcinoma
(according to WHO classification of "Tumours of Rodents")

D. Results
1. Method Establishment: Normal Lung

2. Immunohistology
a) Fixation: Formalin fixation, paraffin embedding, 5 μm sections
b) Antigen Retrieval: Antigen retrieval was performed using a modified method (Table 1).
c) Antibody Incubation: As described in Table 1.

Comparison: Spontaneous Lung Tumors

Adenomat alveolar carcinoma (8901) Fibrolamellar carcinoma (8902) Squamous cell carcinoma (8903) Adenomat alveolar carcinoma (8904) Fibrolamellar carcinoma (8905) Squamous cell carcinoma (8906)

E. Conclusion
The immunohistochemical method described here allows for the identification of CK expression patterns in rat lung tissue and spontaneous rat lung tumors. The results obtained are consistent with previous findings on CK expression in cultured cells and rat lung tissue (Terceira et al., 1993).
